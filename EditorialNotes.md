# Editorial Notes

The Tonal Piano Corpus reproduces the printed sources except in a small number of cases where the notation contains clear internal contradictions. These interventions are minimal, conservative, and fully documented below. The original printed readings are preserved in the TIFF scans.

---

## César Franck — Prelude, Fugue and Variation, Op. 18

### 1. Prelude, bar 104 (right hand)
The printed edition contains an internal contradiction between the repeated figuration in bars 102–105. The right?hand upper voice shows an F? that conflicts with the surrounding pattern and with the harmonic context. The engraved version regularizes the pattern by using F?, matching the parallel bars.

### 2. Fugue, bars 236–237 (left hand)
The printed bass line contains a notational inconsistency in the repeated descending figure. The second iteration omits an accidental that is required by the harmonic sequence. The engraved version restores the missing accidental for internal consistency.
